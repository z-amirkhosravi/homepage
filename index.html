About

My name is Zavosh. I'm an Iranian-born Canadian citizen living in Toronto. Here are some random facts about me:

  • I have a PhD in math from the University of Toronto. My advisor was Stephen S. Kudla. My areas of expertise is the intersection of number theory, algebraic geometry, and representation theory.

  • I've been programming since the 1990s. I started with BASIC, Pascal, C, and Java, but now mostly use C++ and Python.

  • I'm interested in languages. Besides English I'm fluent in Persian, and know French and German at an intermediate level (B1). I also studied Latin and Ancient Greek at university. One day I hope to learn Italian.

  • Two parts of my name have Greek equivalents: Zavosh is Zeus (Ζεὐς) borrowed into Persian, and is a name of the planet Jupiter. Khosrow is Persian cognate to Euclid (Εὐκλείδης) and is two morphemes Kho + srau, meaning "good fame". The word srau is cognate to slav in Slavic languages.
